## Facts and Repeated Actions

### 1. Affirmative Sentences

> Structure: Subject + Verb + Complement

- The _Subject_ is the one that performs the action.
- _Verb_ = action.
- _Complement_ The resto of information in the sentence.

**Examples**:
 
1. We play soccer.
2. I / You / We / They - watch - TV.

#### Third Person Singular
**The third person singular** refers to _he, she, it_ (or singular names/nouns like "the dog," "Maria," "the company"). In the **present simple tense**, when the subject is he/she/it, we add **-s**, **-es**, or **-ies** to the base verb.

| Situation                                  | Rule               | Example                                      |
| ------------------------------------------ | ------------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| Most verbs                                 | add **-s**         | play → play**s**, work → work**s**           |
| Verb ends in -o, -s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -z | add **-es**        | watch → watch**es**, fix → fix**es**         |
| Verb ends in consonant + **y**             | change y → **ies** | study → stud**ies**, fly → fl**ies**         |
| Verb ends in vowel + y                     | just add **-s**    | play → play**s** (no change)                 |
| Irregulars                                 | memorize           | have → **has**, go → **goes**, do → **does** |

**Examples**:

1. He / She / It - watch**es** - TV.
2. He / She / It - eat**s** - breakfast.
3. He / She / It - stud**ies** English.

---
### 2. Negative Sentences

> Structure: Subject + Negative + Verb + Complement

**Examples**:

- We do not (don't) play soccer.
- They do not (don't) like to watch TV.
- She does not (doesn't) go to parties.

---
### 3. Simple Questions

> **Do** + Structure: Subject + Verb + Complement + **?**

- When we make a simple question, the _Third Person Singular_ doesn't impact the verb, foe example:
- 
		- Does he give**s** ... -> Does he **give** ...

**Examples**:
- Do we play soccer?
- Do you speak English?
- Does he give English classes in the afternoons?

#### 3.1 Yes/No Answers
Every time we got a simple question -> _Questions with Do_, we must answer using a Yes/No Answer.

>**Yes/No** + , + Subject + **do/don't**

**Examples**:
- Do we play soccer? -> Yes/No, we do/don't
- Do you speak English? -> Yes/No, I do/don't
- Does he give English classes in the afternoons? -> Yes/No -> he does/doesn't.
