For most retriever quality metrics, these are the most common actors:

1. **Prompt** evaluated for a specific request.
2. Documents took as the **Ranked Result**.
3. **Ground Truth**: All documents labeled as _relevant_ or _irrelevant_ to that question.

## Quality Metrics

1. **Precision**: Measures how many of the returned documents are relevant.
	- `P = Relevant Docs Retrieved / Total Docs Retrieved`
	- Penalizes for returning irrelevant documents.
2. **Recall**: Measures how many of the relevant documents are retrieved.
	- `r = Relevant Docs Retrieved / Total Relevant Docs
	- Penalizes for leaving out relevant documents.

## Reciprocal Ranking

Measures the rank of the first relevant document in the returned list. The later the first relevant is found, the worse the metric would be.

$$ \text{Reciprocal Rank} = \frac{1}{\text{Rank}} $$

-> Mean Reciprocal Ranking (_MRR_) average RR over many prompts.

---
## How to Use Metrics

1. `Recall` and `Recall@K` measures finding relevant documents.
2. `Precision` and `MAP@K` measure search effectiveness.
3. `MRR` measures how well the retriever performs at top ranking
