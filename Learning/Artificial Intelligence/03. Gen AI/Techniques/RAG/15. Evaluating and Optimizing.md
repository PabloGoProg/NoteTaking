## Challenges of RAG in Productions Environments

1. **Scaling Challenges**
	1. More trafic = more latency and load.
	2. More requests = More use of computational resources.
2. **Unpredictability of Prompts**
	1. Prompts will have much more variance when pass to hands of the users.
3. **Messy Real World Data**
	1. Real data is often fragmented, messy or incomplete.
	2. None plain text in most of the cases.
4. **Security and Privacy**
	1. Even if RAGs almost always use private data for retrieval, systems need to safety handle this information on retrieval and augmentation processes.

Mistakes on RAG Systems could create serious _FINANCIAL_ or _REPUTATIONAL_ problem

## Performance Techniques

### 1. Quantization
Quantization is the process of _representing continuous or high-precision values with a smaller set of discrete values_.

In machine learning, quantization usually means converting model weights or activations from high precision formats like FP32 into lower precision formats like FP16, INT8, INT4, or even binary values.

**Benefits of Quantization**:

We can divide the benefits into the two components of [[Transformer Architecture]]:

1. _Embedding Models_: The vector embeddings returned for the embedding models can have _lot of dimensions, representing huge amount of storage memory_ (specially on systems like vector databases). For example **1 million of vectors of 768 dimensions represents 3 GBs of space, while vectors of 4096 dimensions represents 16 GBs**.
2. _LLMs_: LLMs commonly use 16-bit parameters (and having 1 billion to 1 trillion parameters) means huge amounts of memory usage. -> Quantized models shrink the memory footprint.
